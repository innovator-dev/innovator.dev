Factory Process Digitization: MES Platform

Business requirements

In the era of Industry 4.0 and digital transformation, factories are shifting from manual Excel-based tracking to advanced manufacturing execution systems (MES) for end-to-end process management. As manufacturing trends emphasize AI-driven efficiency, industrial IoT, and smart factories in 2026, a MES platform positions providers as leaders in sustainable, data-centric production optimization.

Technical approach

The platform we've engineered for our customer digitizes the entire production workflow, from raw material definition through cutting, blanket creation, printing, to final product assembly, replacing error-prone spreadsheets with real-time visibility and scalability.

Built on a robust tech stack including cloud-native infrastructure, the solution centralizes factory operations with real-time process visibility from material intake to final product dispatch. User management ensures role-based access for operators, supervisors, and admins, while distributed package overviews flag issues instantly for rapid resolution.

Feature highlights

  • User management and access control: Secure multirole authentication with granular permissions for operators, quality teams, and executives to maintain data integrity across teams, ensuring data integrity and controlled access across the factory
  • Product and material definition: Intuitive interfaces for defining raw materials, intermediate products, and final product specifications, tailored to the customer’s production line and processes
  • End-to-end process tracking: Step-by-step digital tracking with timestamps and status updates for full traceability, minimizing errors and enabling bottleneck detection
  • Distributed packages overview: Cloud-native, modular architecture designed to support future extensions such as machine data integration, industrial IoT, automated quality checks, and advanced analytics
